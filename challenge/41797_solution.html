This was the image you were presented. On it we can a see spiral and some rabbits
This should lead you to the so called Fibonacci Sequence which originates from mathematical problem
posed by Leonardo of Pisa (also known as Fibonacci) in his 1202 released book Liber Abaci
This book involves modeling the growth of an idealized rabbit popoulation under some specific assumptions
1. each single new born pair of rabbits (male,female) are put inside a field
2. rabbits are ready to mate at the age of 1 month -> end of second month -> female can produce another pair of rabbits
3. rabbits are immortal, will never stop mating, always create new pair consisting of male and female
So let's suppose the number of rabbits inside that cage at the end of the month is denoted by Fn

source: By Romain - Own work, CC BY-SA 4.0, https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?curid=114416471

Fn=Fn-1+Fn-2

Fn-1: Last Month
Fn-2: Two Months ago This means that the amout of rabbits is calculated: Total this month = Total Last Mth + Two Mth ago

So every new number of the Fibonacci Sequence shows how many pairs of rabbits exist after a given month.

In the background of the picture you can see a spiral this correlates to the golden ratio.
The golden ratio is an irrational number, often symbolized by the greek letter φ (phi) and id approximately equal to 1.618
It appears when a line is divided so that the ratio of the whole to the larger part is the same as the ratio of the
larger part to the smaller. This unique proportion is found throughout nature, art, and architecture, appearing
in the spirals of shells, sunflower seeds, and even in the design of famous structures like the Parthenon.
The golden ratio is also closely related to the Fibonacci sequence, where the ratio of consecutive Fibonacci numbers
gradually approach φ.
