Solution for level 3 - Caesar cipher frequency analysis


You were presented a picture of Asterix and Obelix the lone heroes against Caesar, or are they ?
Caesar used a cipher called, the caesar-cipher (how creative) he used to decrypt the messages he sent.
As seen in the picture below, the alphabet gets moved by a certain amount of characters, to make the message unreadable.
Example:
key: 3
A -> D
B -> E
C -> F

So the text will end up scrambled, however out of modern day view, this method of encryption is not safe anymore.
There are multiple possible ways to solve this level, first one being brute forcing the key until you get a text that makes sense
since there are only 26 possible keys, this would'nt take that long.
However there is another more, thought-through method than guessing with brute force.

The so called Frequency Analysis

where you basically look at the distribution of the letters in the text and then compare them to the englisch language.
In our example the letter h was the most common, which leads to the conclusion, that the key is 3. As seen in the picture below
the letter E is the most common in the englisch alphabet and 3 places away from the letter H, so the key is 3.

Once you did that, you should be presented with the solution

rely on careful analysis. pay attention to letter frequencies and common patterns in the language.
